Private Equity

Let's learn about private equity with Larry

Larry’s Lemonade: Larry Wants Out

Larry has loved building up his business. However, he is getting old, and just isn’t as sharp as he used to be. He wants to sell Larry’s Lemonade. He weighs his options and decides that a private sale would be best. Because his business is so profitable (expensive to buy), only private equity companies are looking into purchasing his business. One in particular is Parker at Parker Capital. Parker is the Managing Director at Parker Capital, and is leading the effort towards acquiring Larry’s Lemonade. He first leads the team in “due diligence”, which includes finding out everything they can about the company to see if it would be a good buy for the price Parker has set. They look at the lemonade market, past growth, profitability, current assets, management, and much more. Parker’s goal is to buy Larry’s Lemonade, make a few changes for further profitability, and then sell it in a few years for a profit.

Private Equity in a Nutshell

  • Private equity firms make money by buying and selling companies
  • Unlike VC firms, PE firms generally buy 100% of mature companies, giving them full control of much larger investments in the 9 to 10 figure range
  • They usually own the company for 5-10 years before selling for a profit

Why Engineers in PE

Private Equity firms make most if not all of their decisions from raw data, whether its information about the company, the competition, or their customer. Engineers love data! We know where to look, what to look for, and how to analyze certain data sets. This is why PE firms love engineers.
